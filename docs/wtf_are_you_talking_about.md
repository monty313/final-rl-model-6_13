## Log

### #001
> **Flag:** "No thinking required — it's pre-decided"
> **What I got wrong:** Said the bot stops thinking at the far ends of the guardrails. It doesn't. It thinks fully — sizes, times, holds, exits. The only thing pre-decided is that it cannot trade *against* the trend the law defines.
> **Correct version:** The bot does the best it can with what it's got, inside the law. The law forbids one direction. Everything else is live judgment.

---

### #002
> **Flag:** "The refine step — guardrails are temporary scaffolding you loosen later"
> **What I got wrong:** Framed the laws as training wheels that get removed once the bot learns. They don't. They are permanent principles — the bot's spine. A law is a law.
> **Correct version:** Guardrails are permanent. The bot lives by them forever. The refine step means the bot gets better at trading *within* the laws, not beyond them.

---

### #003
> **Flag:** "Strict + rare gate — a rail earns 🔴 only if it fires rarely"
> **What I got wrong:** Invented a "rarity" requirement for a law to be permanent. Rarity is irrelevant. A law is a law because it's true, not because it fires rarely.
> **Correct version:** A law earns 🔴 because it defines a trend you will never trade against. Frequency is irrelevant. Remove the rarity gate entirely.

---

### #004
> **Flag:** "The gray zone — tracked quantity, what fraction of training bars are open"
> **What I got wrong:** Built a concept of protecting a "gray middle zone" from being over-railed. That framing was based on the wrong premise (laws as temporary curriculum). Once laws are permanent principles, there is no gray zone to protect — there is only lawful space and the bot thinks freely inside it.
> **Correct version:** Drop the gray-zone tracking concept. The bot operates in whatever space the laws leave open. The laws define the space; the bot fills it.
